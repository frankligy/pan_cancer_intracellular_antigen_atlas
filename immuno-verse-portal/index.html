Manuscript highlights

Hero targets uncovered by ImmunoVerse

Selected discoveries from the manuscript — each has experimental validation and, in several cases, direct evidence of T cell anti-tumor activity.

Alternative splicing · Melanoma

Splicing-derived PMEL (sPMEL)

KTWDQVPFSV · HLA-A*02:01

A novel PMEL peptide produced when exon 5 is skipped, detected in 89% of SKCM samples analyzed. Presented more abundantly and with broader HLA coverage than the canonical clinical targets (KTWGQYWQV, ITDQVPFSV, YLEPGPVTA) despite lower transcript abundance — and with negligible expression in normal skin, offering an enhanced therapeutic window over existing PMEL-directed therapies.

Expanded sPMEL-specific T cells induced potent, specific activation against HLA-A*02:01 / PMEL⁺ melanoma lines, establishing sPMEL as a compelling target for TCRs, PC-CARs, T cell engagers, and peptide vaccines.

Transposable elements · Pan-cancer

LINE1 ORF2 as a pan-cancer target

RIAKSILSQK · ILPKVIYRF · SGYKINVQK

The first reported evidence of native LINE1 ORF2 protein from endogenous expression across multiple cancers — detected on HLA across 12 tumor types, with highest frequency in COAD, HNSC, and lung cancers. ORF2 drives LINE1/Alu retrotransposition and genomic instability and has historically evaded Western blot and whole-cell proteomics.

Three ORF2 peptides were synthesized, confirmed by LC-MS/MS, and shown to be tumor-specific versus normal immunopeptidomes, nominating ORF2 as a broadly applicable pan-cancer immunotherapy target.

Microbial antigens · Ovarian

N. circulans in 98% of ovarian tumors

Niallia circulans · C. ureolyticus

A stringent k-mer re-classification pipeline revealed highly recurrent tumor-resident microbes in gynecologic cancers — most notably Niallia circulans, detected in up to 98% of ovarian tumors across independent collection sites after alignment to the FDA-submitted reference genome. Additional hits include H. pylori (STAD), F. nucleatum (COAD/SKCM/HNSC/ESCA), HBV, HPV, and CMV.

CMV peptides were natively presented on HLA across 7 tumor types — the first evidence of endogenously presented CMV epitopes in primary human tumors — opening a microbial axis for immunotherapy.

Cryptic ORFs · Non-canonical

Cryptic ORFs dominate non-canonical peptides

LYLETRSEF · AYPASLQTL

Cryptic (nuORF) derived antigens contribute 69% of the non-canonical immunopeptidome on average. Ribo-Seq across eight neuroblastoma PDXs confirmed ribosomal occupancy on 62% of these ORF-derived pHLAs, validating active translation. LYLETRSEF (POLR2K 5' UTR) and AYPASLQTL (C19orf48P pseudogene) were confirmed by LC-MS/MS and pHLA refolding, with LYLETRSEF recurrent across SKCM, DLBC, GBM, HNSC, and BRCA.

Self-antigens · Pan-cancer

MAGE-A4 across 13 cancers; under-explored PRAME variant

GVYDGREHTV · GQHLHLETF

MAGE-A4 GVYDGREHTV (HLA-A*02:01) — the target of an FDA-approved TCR-T in synovial sarcoma — was observed across 13 cancer types. A previously under-explored PRAME peptide GQHLHLETF (HLA-B*15:01) was detected in 67% of B*15:01 carriers across seven cancers, compared with 27% detection of the canonical PRAME target SLLQHLIGL among A*02:01 carriers — and with higher relative abundance — expanding the actionable PRAME repertoire.

Mutation neoantigens

2,401 empirically detected neoantigens

KRAS G12V · CTNNB1 T41A · NPM1 frameshift

A histology-matched search across the curated TCGA/TARGET mutation catalogue recovered 2,401 immunopeptidome-evidenced neoantigens, 96% of them not previously documented in IEDB. Hits include well-characterized KRAS G12V (VVVGAVGVGK), NPM1 frameshift (AVEEVSLRK), CKAP2 frameshift (SLMEQIPHL), plus new shared neoantigens in LARP4B (colon) and CTNNB1 (liver). ImmunoVerse includes a fast-turnaround module for integrating this step into cancer vaccine pipelines.

Methods · Pipeline

How ImmunoVerse was built

An integrative multimodal pipeline combining transcriptomic events, tri-engine immunopeptidomic search, stringent multi-modal safety filtering, and single-cell essentiality analysis.

01
Catalogue
11 classes of tumor events

7,188 tumor RNA-Seq + 17,384 GTEx normals assembled into a histology-specific catalogue of canonical and non-canonical sources — filtered against 51 normal tissues.

02
Search
Tri-engine immunopeptidomics

1,823 immunopeptidomes searched with MaxQuant, MS2Rescore, and AI-driven Tesorai (500M+ spectra). Tesorai boosted IDs by an average of 125% over MaxQuant.

03
Safety
Multimodal safety screen

Candidates screened against the HLA Ligand Atlas (752 normal runs, 30 tissues) and a Ribo-Seq healthy cryptic ORF database — excluding an additional 19% with normal-tissue evidence.

04
Binding
NetMHCpan 4.1 prediction

Predicted HLA binding layered onto empirical detection wherever HLA typing was available, excluding non-binders across all three search engines.

05
Prioritize
Therapeutic annotations

Each peptide scored on abundance, recurrence, normal-tissue exclusion, HLA affinity, DepMap essentiality, scRNA homogeneity, and MS confidence.

06
Deploy
Code-free cloud pipeline

Entire pipeline available as a code-free cloud workflow on Cancer Genomics Cloud — run your own data through ImmunoVerse in under an hour.
